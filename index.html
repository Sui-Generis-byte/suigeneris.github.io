History of Abacha

Abacha, also called African Salad, is a traditional delicacy from southeastern Nigeria. It is usually made from dried shredded cassava, mixed with palm oil, spices, vegetables, and sometimes fish. Its preparation has been passed down for generations, symbolizing both culture and culinary skill.

The Effort Behind Every Plate

Making Abacha requires patience and attention to detail. From soaking and shredding the cassava, frying the components lightly, to carefully blending spices and vegetables, each plate reflects the care and dedication of the maker. The balance of flavors, textures, and colors is what makes every bite memorable.

Ingredients Breakdown

  • Dried shredded cassava (main base)
  • Palm oil
  • Potash (for texture)
  • Crayfish powder
  • Garden eggs / Ugba (oil bean seeds)
  • Onions (purple accent)
  • Spices: pepper, salt, seasoning cubes
  • Protein (optional): stockfish, dry fish, or meat
  • Vegetables for garnish: utazi leaves, garden eggs
